#TweetWars 



Knock your opponent off the field.

... because that was easy to write. Looking for more game rule ideas!

Read below for a summary of how to write out a strategy to control the players.
Right now you have to break out the expression onto four lines for each player
(the input boxes above), but the plan is to have them copy-pasteable for
tweeting/sharing purposes.

This game is under active development. I love feedback! I'm @unicyclephil.


## Physics
Field radius: 1 metre
Player mass: 6 Kg
Player radius: 3 cm
Force: [evaluation of strategy expression] Newtons (capped at 1)
Integrator: Runge Kutta
Collisions: fully elastic


## Forces you control:
r<  push toward centre.
r^  push tangent to centre.
o<  push directly toward opponent.
o^  push counter-clockwise around opponent. Likely to
change.


## Variables available to you:
r  your distance from the centre (m)
r' your speed toward the centre (m/s)
r| your speed tangential to the center (m/s)
o  distance to your opponent
o' speed toward opponent (relative to them)
o| speed tangential to opponent (relative to them)


## Math operators available to you (in order of precedence):
( ) [ ] control order of operations. square brackets return the absolute value.
^     raise to power
+  -  unary plus and minus. plus does... effectively nothing. minus negates the
value following it.
*  /  multiply, divide
+  -  add, subtract
<  >  less-than, greater-than (evaluate to 1.0 or 0.0 for true/false)


## Math Bugs:
float literals  ".2" evaluates to 2. The lexer is
eating leading decimals. Workaround: zero prefix — "0.2".

## Missing Math:
Convenient Constants  e, pi, etc.
Brackets  Parentheses are currently a syntax error. You cannot
control order of operations (yet!).
Unary minus  "-5" is currently a syntax error. Use 0-5.
Absolute value  Not sure how I want this too look yet.
